What you get for your specific CRM

Integration depth varies by CRM. We support three levels — full API (Level 3), optimized scraping (Level 2), and generic scan mode (Level 1) — so the extension does as much as possible for every CRM, not the lowest common denominator.

HubSpot Level 3 · Full API

  • Launch from any contact, company, or deal list — pick records and dial
  • Launch from saved HubSpot lists (up to 500 records per session)
  • Launch from your HubSpot task queue — tasks auto-complete after each call
  • Follow widget auto-navigates to the active contact's HubSpot record
  • Call activities log automatically via the PhoneBurner ↔ HubSpot integration
See the full HubSpot integration on phoneburner.com →

Close Level 3 · Full API

  • Launch dial sessions from Close contacts or leads
  • Automatic phone and email fetching via the Close API
  • Call activities logged back to Close, including disposition and recording link
  • Notes typed during calls flow into the Close call activity
  • OAuth authentication — connect securely with one click

Apollo.io Level 3 · Full API

  • Launch dial sessions from the Apollo People page — select contacts and dial
  • Dial from sequence call tasks — pick a sequence, filter by due tasks, power-dial
  • Filter tasks by due today, overdue, or all open
  • Auto-navigate to Apollo contact profiles during calls
  • OAuth authentication — connect securely with one click

Salesforce Level 2 · Optimized

  • Optimized page scraping tuned for Salesforce list views
  • Select rows (checkbox) and launch a dial session for the selected contacts
  • Follow widget keeps the Salesforce tab in sync with the active call
  • No OAuth required — works the moment you load a list view

Pipedrive Level 2 · Optimized

  • Optimized page scraping for the Pipedrive Persons list
  • Dial all visible person rows with one click
  • Follow widget tracks the active contact in Pipedrive during the session
  • No OAuth required

Zoho Level 1 · Generic

  • Generic page-scan mode extracts visible contact details from list views
  • Dial through whatever the page shows — adapts to most Zoho layouts
  • Follow widget navigation works for most page structures

monday.com Level 1 · Generic

  • Generic page-scan mode reads contact details from board/table views
  • Launch dial sessions from monday.com tables that include phone fields
  • Follow widget integrates with the monday.com tab during sessions

The generic page-scan mode works on most table or list-based CRM pages — even ones we haven't specifically optimized for. Install the extension and try it on your CRM. If you're a larger team that wants deeper Level 3 support for a specific system, get in touch — we add new providers regularly.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a PhoneBurner account?

Yes. The extension launches dial sessions through your existing PhoneBurner subscription — it doesn't replace PhoneBurner, it makes your CRM able to drive it. If you don't have an account yet, start a free trial at phoneburner.com.

How is my PhoneBurner credential stored? Is it secure?

Yes — and more carefully than most extensions. When you save your Personal Access Token, it's transmitted over HTTPS to PhoneBurner-managed servers and stored with strict owner-only file permissions (mode 0600). Your browser does NOT keep a copy of the token. The browser only stores an anonymous lookup key (a random UUID); the actual token lives server-side. This means a compromised browser profile or another extension can't read your PhoneBurner credentials.

How do call logs get into my CRM?

It depends on the CRM:

  • HubSpot: Call activities log via the existing PhoneBurner ↔ HubSpot integration in your PhoneBurner account (activate it once under PhoneBurner Settings → Integrations → HubSpot).
  • Close: Call activities log automatically through the extension itself — including disposition, custom outcome, and recording link.
  • Apollo: Activities log automatically through the extension, including call notes.
  • Salesforce, Pipedrive, Zoho, monday.com, others: Call activity logging isn't currently supported for these CRMs by the extension. PhoneBurner's main app retains full call records.
Will it work for my whole team?

Yes. Each rep installs the extension on their own Chrome profile, pastes their own PhoneBurner Personal Access Token, and connects their own CRM credentials via OAuth. For HubSpot specifically, the rep needs App Marketplace Access in their HubSpot user permissions — your HubSpot admin can grant it under Settings → Users & Teams → [user] → Access → Edit Permissions → Settings access → App Marketplace access.

How much does the extension cost?

The extension itself is free. You only pay for your PhoneBurner subscription. See PhoneBurner pricing.

What if my CRM isn't listed?

Try it anyway. The extension's generic page-scan mode works on most table or list-based CRM pages. If you're a larger team and want deeper Level 3 support for a specific CRM, reach out — we add new providers regularly.

What permissions does the extension request and why?
  • storage — to persist your preferences and the anonymous lookup key for your PhoneBurner credential.
  • activeTab — to detect when you're on a supported CRM page (read the URL).
  • scripting — to inject the contact-scanner ONLY when you click Launch.
  • tabs — to keep the Follow widget in sync with the right CRM tab during a call.
  • permissions — to request optional CRM host access at runtime when you initiate a dial session.
  • host permissions for phoneburner.biz — to talk to our backend API.

No content script runs and no CRM data is read until you explicitly initiate a dial session. Nothing is collected passively.

Does PhoneBurner build and maintain this?

Yes — the PhoneBurner Dial Session Companion is built and maintained by PhoneBurner. For support, see our knowledge base or email jeff@phoneburner.com.
